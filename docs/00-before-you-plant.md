# Before You Plant

**Read this before deploying ANY TreeGuard unit.**

## Legal checklist

- [ ] **Permission:** Do you have written permission to plant on this land? Public land requires permits from your municipality. Private land requires landowner consent.
- [ ] **Utility lines:** Call before you dig. In the US: dial 811. In the EU: check your national service. Hitting a gas line kills people.
- [ ] **Species selection:** Is this species native or approved for your area? Check your regional invasive species list. Planting invasives causes ecological damage regardless of good intentions.
- [ ] **Water source:** Is there a realistic water source nearby? TreeGuard relies on passersby with bottles. A tree on a remote trail with zero foot traffic will die.

## Site selection

**Good sites:**
- Sidewalks, parks, school grounds with foot traffic
- Near water fountains or shops (people carry bottles)
- Partial sun minimum (solar panel needs light)
- Visible from a path (LED must be seen)

**Bad sites:**
- Deep shade under canopy (solar panel starves; sensor reads wet from canopy drip)
- Flood zones (sensor always reads wet)
- High-vandalism areas with no community buy-in (deploy community program first, hardware second)
- Rocky ground where sensor probe can't reach 20 cm depth

## Sensor placement

- Insert the capacitive probe **20-30 cm deep**, adjacent to the rootball, NOT through it
- The probe measures the moisture available to roots, not surface wetness
- Surface soil dries fast in sun — a shallow sensor gives false "thirsty" readings

## Species matters

Different trees need different moisture levels. Check [02-species-presets.md](02-species-presets.md) and set the correct preset before deployment. A willow and an olive have completely different thirst profiles.

## Your responsibility

Deploying a TreeGuard means committing to:
1. **Monthly check-ins** for the first year (battery, sensor, frame integrity)
2. **Expanding or removing the frame** as the tree grows (year 2-4 typically)
3. **Reporting data** back to the project (anonymized survival logs help everyone)
4. **Removing dead units** — a broken blinking box is litter, not conservation
