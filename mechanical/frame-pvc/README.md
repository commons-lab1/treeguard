# PVC Pipe Frame

The cheapest and fastest build option. No 3D printer needed.

## Materials

| Part | Spec | Qty | Cost |
|------|------|-----|------|
| PVC pipe | 32 mm OD, schedule 20 | 2.1 m (cut into 3× 700mm) | ~$2 |
| PP mesh or chicken wire | 10mm grid, ~0.5m × 1m roll | 1 | ~$3 |
| Cable ties (UV-resistant) | 200 mm, black | 20 | ~$1 |
| Ground stakes | 10 mm rebar, 250 mm long | 3 | ~$1 |
| Weatherproof junction box | 80×80×50 mm, IP65 | 1 | ~$2 |
| **Total** | | | **~$9** |

## Tools

- Hacksaw or pipe cutter
- Wire cutters (if using chicken wire)
- Drill + 10mm bit
- Marker

## Build steps

### 1. Cut uprights
Cut PVC pipe into 3 pieces, each 700 mm long.

### 2. Drill stake sockets
Drill a 10 mm hole in the bottom of each upright, 30 mm deep (or just friction-fit over rebar).

### 3. Arrange uprights
Place uprights in a triangle, 200 mm apart (center to center). Push ground stakes into soil, slide uprights over stakes.

### 4. Wrap mesh
Cut PP mesh / chicken wire to wrap around the three uprights forming a triangular cage. Leave the top open for tree growth.

Secure mesh to uprights with UV-resistant cable ties every 100-150 mm.

### 5. Mount electronics
- Drill 3× 6mm cable gland holes in the junction box
- Mount PCB inside with hot glue or standoffs
- Route sensor wire down one upright (tape or cable-tie to pipe)
- Route LED wires to the outside face of the box
- Mount junction box on one upright at ~500 mm height using cable ties
- Seal cable glands with silicone

### 6. Deploy
Follow [deployment checklist](../../docs/03-deployment-checklist.md).

## Notes

- Ugly but functional — this is for rapid field testing, not beauty contests
- PVC degrades in UV over years — paint with outdoor acrylic if longevity matters
- PP mesh is preferred over metal chicken wire (no rust, lighter, won't cut hands)
