# Laser-Cut Plywood Frame

A flat-pack frame cut from 6 mm plywood. Ships flat, assembles in 5 minutes with no tools.

## Materials

| Part | Spec | Qty | Cost |
|------|------|-----|------|
| Marine plywood | 6 mm, 600×400 mm sheet | 2 | ~$5 |
| Outdoor wood sealer | Polyurethane or marine varnish | 1 can | ~$5 (shared across many units) |
| Wood glue (optional) | Exterior grade | — | ~$1 |
| **Total per unit** | | | **~$6** |

## Design

The frame consists of:
- **3× side panels** (600 mm tall × ~200 mm wide) with ventilation cutouts
- **3× base tabs** that slot into the side panels and anchor with ground stakes
- **1× electronics bracket** that slots into one side panel

All connections are **finger joints** — no fasteners needed for assembly. Wood glue optional for permanence.

## Laser settings (6mm plywood)

| Laser type | Power | Speed | Passes |
|------------|-------|-------|--------|
| CO2 40W | 90% | 8 mm/s | 1 |
| CO2 60W | 70% | 12 mm/s | 1 |
| Diode 10W | 100% | 2 mm/s | 3 |

## Assembly

1. Slot 3 side panels together via finger joints into a triangular prism
2. Insert base tabs at the bottom of each panel
3. Push ground stakes through base tab holes into soil
4. Clip electronics bracket into the slot on one panel
5. Mount electronics box to bracket

## Finishing

**Apply 2 coats of outdoor sealer before deployment.** Unsealed plywood will delaminate within one rainy season.

Coat cut edges especially — they absorb water fastest.

## Notes

- Heavier than 3D-printed or PVC versions
- More "natural" aesthetic — good for parks and residential areas
- Biodegradable at end of life (leave sealer to wear off, wood decomposes)
- Not suitable for very wet climates without excellent sealing
